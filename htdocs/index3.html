Effects of Global Warming

Global warming makes it harder for organisms to survive on this planet. For humans, global warming increases severe weather events and strikes harder against people who don't have much access to food, water, or shelter. For other organisms, global warming destroys ecosystems or habitats where diverse wildlife resides.

1. More Severe Weather
The rise in the atmospheric temperature due to global warming increases the occurrence of heat waves and droughts. More droughts also increase the chances of wildfires in forests. The increase in global temperature also causes glaciers to melt. This causes a rise in the sea level, which in turn may cause more floods, storm surges, or typhoons.
2. Biodiversity loss
In the oceans, a rise in sea level and temperature disturbs marine ecosystems by killing corals. Higher than usual temperatures cause algae to leave corals, causing the latter to turn white and eventually die, in a process called coral bleaching. Without corals, other marine organisms lose their homes and cannot survive properly. In polar ecosystems, animals lose their homes when the sea ice melts, because, without this, the animals are unable to travel and look for food. Other weather problems like droughts and forest fires do not only affect humans, but they can also kill plants and make animals lose their homes.
3. More Dangers to Human Health
First of all, some causes of global warming, like air pollution, pose a great health risk to people. Food and water sources are also affected by severe weather. Floods allow an increased spread of mosquito-borne diseases like malaria and dengue viruses. Higher temperatures also contribute to the spread of many types of diseases, like Cholera, Tuberculosis, and Lyme Disease.
