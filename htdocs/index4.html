Preventing Global Warming

There are a lot of things people can do to prevent or at least slow down global warming. The ways to prevent global warming are derived from identifying the causes of it, then finding ways to counter the causes. However, not all people will be able to act as much about global warming, mainly due to the lack of funds, but those who are able to act against it should try their best to do so.

1. Switch to Renewable Energy
Changing energy sources and switching to a renewable energy source can greatly reduce the amount of air pollutants and greenhouse gases released into the atmosphere. Some examples of such energy sources are solar, geothermal, and hydroelectric energy, and using these as our sources of energy can spare our environment from all the pollutants produced from unrenewable energy sources.
2. Save Electricity
While unrenewable energy sources are still widely used, the least we can do is save electricity. This electricity may be for lighting, heating, or other purposes. Of course, using less electricity by avoiding unnecessary usage of appliances will lessen the need for energy production, and therefore lessening the pollution of the air and the environment.
3. Conserve Materials
The manufacturing process for most, if not all, of the things we use, have contributed to greenhouse gas emissions at some point. It may be with the shipping of raw materials or the processing in factories. Trying to utilize the most out of these products will surely help in reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
4. Change Mode of Transportation
Most modes of transportation also contribute to air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. Using alternative modes of transportation like walking, cycling, or using public transport can lessen this. Another option is using electric cars, which use electricity that might come from harmful sources, but are still better for the environment than cars that use gas. However, this is only feasible for those who have sufficient funds to purchase one.
5. Reduce Food Waste
The livestock and agricultural industries contribute a lot to our carbon emissions. Approximately one-third of food made isn't used at all and is thrown away instead. When food is thrown away to rot, it also emits more harmful gasses. So, making sure that as much food as possible is used to feed others and is not thrown away will surely help the environment.
