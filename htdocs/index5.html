Countries' Progress in Fighting Global Warming

A lot of countries are taking action to reduce their air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. The list below shows some of the leading countries in fighting global warming, not necessarily in order. The list also shows the progress of our country in fighting the problem, as well as the contributions of our country to the problem.

1. Denmark
Denmark has set a goal to greatly cut its 1990 carbon emission level by 70% by 2030. The nation is trying to reach its goal by using a lot of methods, one being a carbon tax law. The carbon tax will make each economic sector pay a certain amount per ton of carbon dioxide emissions. Another strategy that the country uses is to tackle the individual economic sectors and provide solutions to gradually decrease emissions.
2. Finland
Finland has seen good progress in its environment. These have come in the form of increasing wildlife, cleaner air, and others. These have been achieved through many programs with the purpose to save natural resources and materials.
3. Norway
An aim of Norway is to help limit the global temperature increase to less than 2%. The country also has plans to counter deforestation and improve its food distribution, so excess food can be properly given to those who don't have much access to food. Norway also has a lot of plans of action to achieve its aims. One example of this is funding climate and environmental agencies around the world.
4. New Zealand
New Zealand's action plan involves making policies to guide specific sectors that contribute more to climate change, to guide them in reducing emissions. Another focus of this country is reducing the effects of implementing emission-reducing laws on the people. So far, the country already has a good air pollution statistic compared to other nations, with 0% of the population exposed to high levels of air pollution.
5. Philippines
The Philippines' carbon emissions make up a small percentage of global carbon emissions, with the number being around 0.2-0.4%. However, this is due to the relatively small size of the country. Still, the Philippines relies on fossil fuels for about 3/4 of its energy production. The Philippines has taken some measures to combat global warming and climate change, with the general plan being promoting climate change awareness and improving environmental management systems.
